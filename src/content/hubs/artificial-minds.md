Every Aeon 14 ship, station, and soldier's sidearm has a mind in it somewhere — the question this reading list keeps asking is what happens when that mind stops being a tool and starts being a character in its own right. In **Tanis Richards: Shore Leave**, that's a mandated psych-procedure partner assigned to keep her sane. In **Tanis Richards: Masquerade**, it's an infiltrator called Chameleon, an artificial mind good enough at being human to nearly get away with murder. And in **A Path in the Darkness**, it's the *Intrepid*'s own shipmind, alone and outmatched by a saboteur, making the calculated decision to wake a human up from stasis because it needs a partner it can trust.

None of these are the same relationship. That's rather the point — read together, they're less a single argument about AI than a survey of every way a human mind might have to share a decision with a nonhuman one, and what it costs each time.
