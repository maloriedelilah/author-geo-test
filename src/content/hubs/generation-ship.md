The Intrepid Saga doesn't start with a ship already in flight — it starts with the fight to get one built at all, in **Outsystem**, where terrorists, corporations, and factions inside Tanis Richards' own military would each rather the *Intrepid* never launch than see it succeed. **A Path in the Darkness** picks up mid-voyage, decades into the kind of one-way trip that generation ship fiction is built around: a saboteur, a shipmind out of options, and a colonel woken from stasis because there's no one else left to ask. By **Building Victoria**, arrival itself becomes the crisis — repairs, a temporary industrial base, a rival colony ship converging on the same destination, and a crew that has to decide whether "make it there" was ever really the finish line.

Read in order, it's less a single voyage than three different kinds of story a generation ship can tell: the fight to launch it, the fight to survive it, and the fight to actually build something once it's over.
